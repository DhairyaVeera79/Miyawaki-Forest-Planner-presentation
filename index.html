Miyawaki Forest Planner

A practical and mission-aligned planning platform to standardize Miyawaki afforestation execution, improve survival outcomes, and deliver transparent ecological impact communication.

01

The problem you are solving

SRLC’s afforestation seva has strong intent and impact, but execution quality becomes uneven without a central planning and monitoring system as project volume increases.

Current execution pain

  • Site planning relies on manual judgment without a standardized digital flow.
  • Species selection and layer balancing are inconsistent across locations.
  • Sapling quantity, buffers, procurement planning, and inventory discipline vary by team.
  • Field SOP guidance is not always delivered in one guided sequence for new volunteers.
  • Monitoring cadence, photo evidence quality, and survival tracking are uneven.

Why this is critical now

  • Fixed delivery dates require scope discipline with reliable operational execution.
  • Leadership needs auditable records, risk visibility, and portfolio-level confidence.
  • Donor and beneficiary communication needs verified, evidence-backed impact pathways.
  • Without structure, coordination overhead and rework can reduce ecological outcomes.

Core problem statement

The current afforestation operating model is not consistently scalable. SRLC needs a unified, role-aware system that standardizes planning, procurement, field execution, monitoring, and impact reporting while preserving human ecological accountability.

02

The proposed solution

Miyawaki Forest Planner is the end-to-end internal operating system for site selection, four-layer native planning, procurement, SOP execution, monitoring intelligence, and approved impact storytelling.

Strategic objectives

  • Standardize end-to-end Miyawaki project execution across geographies.
  • Improve survival outcomes through better planning and monitoring discipline.
  • Reduce field rework and coordinator overhead.
  • Enable donor-ready transparency through structured evidence records.

In scope by core build

  • Map polygon planning with revision history and metadata extraction.
  • Native species and four-layer planning with expert override.
  • Density-based quantity and PO-ready procurement outputs.
  • SOP-driven execution, monitoring cadence, and governance dashboards.

Post-deadline scope

  • Water-body, path, fireline, and topography-aware optimization.
  • 3D explorer and species-only visual mode.
  • Sapling-level identity at scale and advanced vendor intelligence.
  • Enhanced donor traceability experiences.

Site Planner

Polygon draw and re-draw, centroid and coordinate capture, region context detection, and full boundary audit history.

Ecological Planning

India-first native shortlist mapped to canopy, tree, sub-tree, and shrub layers with practical local adaptability.

Quantity & Procurement

Density sensitivity, survival-risk buffers, species-wise quantities, vendor sheets, and purchase-order-ready output.

Inventory Baseline

IMS-lite tracking for required, ordered, received, planted, and replacement-buffer sapling counts.

Layout Blueprint

Spacing recommendations, geometry variants, and practical density caps to avoid non-viable plantation patterns.

SOP & Readiness

Guided checklists for soil prep, natural inputs, plantation sequence, watering cadence, protection, and early care.

Monitoring Intelligence

Timestamped photo updates by capture points, reminders, risk flags, advisory stress alerts, and maturity forecasting.

Dashboard & Reporting

Project and portfolio visibility for phase status, update compliance, survival trends, and approved impact stories.

Recommended Geospatial Implementation Stack

  • Map UI: Leaflet + Leaflet-Geoman for robust polygon draw/edit/snap and lasso-style workflows.
  • Basemap strategy: OpenStreetMap tiles for baseline plus satellite-capable providers for site verification.
  • Client-side geospatial: Turf.js for geometry checks and quick advisory analytics.
  • Backend geospatial truth: PostGIS for projection-safe area calculations and audit-safe geometry storage.
  • Scale-up path: MapLibre GL JS for high-performance vector rendering in future advanced visual modes.

Workflow Blueprint (Authoritative 9-Step Flow)

Phase 1 • Intake and Planning

  1. Create project and confirm site ownership context.
  2. Draw or revise boundary polygon and capture metadata.
  3. Set density objectives and generate native four-layer plan.

Phase 2 • Execution and Monitoring

  1. Generate quantities, procurement sheets, and layout guidance.
  2. Run SOP-guided field execution with natural-input-first readiness.
  3. Capture periodic photos and metrics with reminder-driven cadence.

Phase 3 • Outcomes and Governance

  1. Surface phase status, delays, and risk flags in dashboards.
  2. Forecast maturity window and transition-to-self-sustaining trajectory.
  3. Publish approved donor and beneficiary impact narratives from verified evidence.

Primary users

  • Field Sevaks (C4/C3): execute tasks, follow SOPs, upload updates and media evidence.

Secondary beneficiaries

Key technical or operational dependencies

Delivery requires aligned operational ownership, data discipline, platform readiness, and clear governance boundaries for risk-controlled execution.

Technical dependencies

  • Authentication, role-based authorization, and auditable change history.
  • Reliable storage and media pipeline for monitoring evidence.
  • Notification channels for reminders and pending-action workflows.
  • Reporting stack for project and portfolio-level analytics visibility.
  • Advisory AI integration with transparency and confidence signaling.

Operational dependencies

  • Local ecological validation partners and reviewer availability.
  • Nursery and vendor ecosystem mapping by geography.
  • SOP ownership, field training accountability, and participation continuity.
  • Consistent monitoring cadence commitment across project teams.
  • Community participation model with dignified engagement opportunities.

Data and analytics dependencies

  • Regional native species references and lifecycle context.
  • Climate and soil context where available.
  • Historical plantation outcomes for calibration where available.
  • Field media and metadata quality discipline for reliable insights.

AI boundaries and safety

  • AI is assistive for planning, summarization, and forecasting.
  • AI does not make irreversible ecological or publication decisions.
  • Human and expert review required before external communication.
  • Disease and maturity conclusions require ecological validation.

Risk and mitigation

  • Species mismatch risk: mandatory expert checkpoint before plan lock.
  • Monitoring inconsistency: cadence reminders and escalation views.
  • Volunteer turnover: SOP continuity and project memory in-system.
  • Reporting accuracy risk: publish only approved and verified evidence.

Governance and ethics standards

  • Use respectful, non-commercial mission language in all communication.
  • Keep beneficiary and local community narratives dignified and non-extractive.
  • Maintain clear human accountability in ecological and governance decisions.
  • Preserve culturally accurate and reverential framing where required.

Core completion acceptance

  • Pilot project executes from site selection through procurement outputs.
  • SOP-guided execution works for new teams without external dependency gaps.
  • Monitoring entries flow into dashboard risk and leadership visibility.
  • Approved data converts into donor and beneficiary impact narratives.
